Crime Data Analysis - 2025

Introduction to the Crime Dataset

The Crime Incidents in 2025 dataset provides a comprehensive overview of criminal activities recorded in a metropolitan area throughout the year. The dataset includes essential information such as:

  • Crime Type (OFFENSE): Categorizes offenses such as theft, robbery, and assault.
  • Time & Shift: Identifies whether crimes occurred in the day, evening, or midnight shift.
  • Method Used: Specifies the means of crime (e.g., gun, knife, or other tools).
  • Location Details: Includes latitude, longitude, district, and ward classifications.
  • Timestamp: Captures the exact date and time of reported crimes.

This dataset enables detailed crime trend analysis, hotspot detection, and predictive modeling to assist law enforcement in resource allocation and crime prevention.

Conclusion - Key Findings from Visualizations

1️.Crime Trends Over Time

  • Crime incidents exhibit seasonal patterns, with peak activity during specific periods.
  • A 7-day rolling average reveals fluctuations in crime frequency.

2️. Crime Distribution by Type & Method

  • Theft and auto-related crimes dominate the dataset.
  • Most crimes are committed using non-weapon methods, but firearms and knives are linked to serious offenses.

3️. Geospatial Crime Hotspots

  • K-Means & DBSCAN clustering highlight high-risk areas.
  • Crime is concentrated in specific geographic zones, requiring targeted policing.

4️. Crime Timing & Predictability

  • Crime activity peaks in the evening (15:00 - 23:00).
  • Autocorrelation analysis suggests periodic trends but also randomness, making long-term forecasting challenging.

5️. Anomaly Detection

  • Isolation Forest detected unusual crime patterns that require further monitoring.
  • Anomalies may indicate emerging crime trends or isolated incidents.

Final Takeaways

  • Crime hotspots are concentrated in certain areas, necessitating proactive policing.
  • Crime rates fluctuate based on the time of day, with high activity during evenings.
  • Weapon-involved crimes (e.g., guns, knives) are associated with high-severity offenses.
  • Unexpected crime anomalies suggest potential shifts in crime trends.

Understanding these patterns can help law enforcement, policymakers, and urban planners take proactive steps towards improving public safety.
